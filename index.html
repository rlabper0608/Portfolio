Biography

Beginnings and Freestyle Career

Walls, whose real name is Ginés Paredes Giménez, was born on September 10, 2000, in Murcia, Spain. His stage name comes from the English translation of his last name: Paredes →Walls. From a very young age, he became interested in rap and improvisation, taking part in local freestyle competitions where he quickly stood out for his wit, flow, and charisma on stage.

His rise to fame came through his participation in Freestyle Master Series (FMS) Spain, the countryʼs top professional rap battle league. There, he achieved second place in his first season and established himself as one of the most promising names in the scene. His style combined lyrical sharpness with emotional delivery, making him one of the most beloved freestylers of his generation.

Transition from Freestyle to Music

Despite his success in freestyle, Walls decided to make a radical change in his career to focus on his true passion: music. Around 2019, he announced his retirement from competitive freestyle to fully dedicate himself to songwriting and performing. His goal was to create a more personal and emotional artistic project, far from the competitive atmosphere of freestyle.

During this period, he began releasing his first songs on digital platforms, showcasing a blend of rap, pop, and urban sounds. Tracks like Por partes and Complicao marked the beginning of a new artistic path, revealing a young artist searching for his own sound and identity.

Breakthrough and the Release of LNDP

In 2022, Walls released his debut studio album, “Los niños del parqueˮ (“The Kids from the Parkˮ), a project filled with youthful energy, emotion, and nostalgia. The album was well-received, establishing him as a solid figure in the Spanish pop-urban scene, while keeping his authenticity and emotional tone intact.

This project represented a turning point in his career, allowing him to embark on a national tour across Spain and perform at major music festivals, where he showcased his versatility as a performer and songwriter.

Evolution Toward Pop-Rock and a New Artistic Stage

In recent years, Walls has evolved toward a more pop-rock and indie sound, exploring new sonic textures and more introspective lyrics. His single “A mi laoˮ (2023) symbolized this artistic transformation, introducing a more mature aesthetic and refined musical direction.

In 2024, he released his project “LUNA 18ˮ, an album that solidifies this evolution — blending alternative rock influences with melodic pop and polished production. With this release, Walls has left behind the label of “freestylerˮ to become one of the leading voices in the new wave of Spanish pop music.

Albums

38º

38º marked a transitional phase in Wallsʼ career, moving away from his freestyle roots toward a more melodic, structured, and emotional sound. The EP blends pop and urban influences while preserving some rap DNA, showing the first glimpse of the artist he would later become.

Los Niños del Parque

This debut album represented Wallsʼ official transition from freestyler to mainstream artist. Los Niños del Parque captures youth, nostalgia, and self-discovery through a fusion of pop, indie, and urban influences. The album solidified his reputation as one of the leading voices in Spainʼs new pop scene.

Luna 18

LUNA 18 represents Wallsʼ artistic reinvention, embracing rock, pop-rock, and punk-inspired sounds. The album features live instrumentation, electric guitars, and emotionally charged lyrics, reflecting maturity and confidence in his artistry. Itʼs considered his boldest and most personal project to date.

Luna 18 (Deluxe)

This expanded edition reinforces the narrative of LUNA 18, showcasing Wallsʼ growth as a songwriter and performer. It blends rock, pop, and emotional introspection, further defining his evolution from freestyle prodigy to full-fledged pop-rock artist.
